Questions I Had 
1. How to Identify Whether a Stock is Bullish or Bearish? 
2. Why is Volume Important During a Breakout? 
3. How Do Support and Resistance Help Traders? 
 Answers 
- A stock is considered bullish when it forms: Higher Highs, Higher Lows. 
A stock is considered bearish when it forms: Lower Highs, Lower Lows. 
Trend structure helps traders understand market direction and momentum. 
- Volume confirms the strength of a breakout. 
If price breaks resistance with high volume, it indicates strong buying 
participation and increases the reliability of the move. 
Low-volume breakouts are weaker and may fail. 
- Support and resistance help traders identify: 
Possible entry points, Exit zones, Reversal areas, Breakout levels. 
Support acts like a floor where price may bounce upward, while resistance acts 
like a ceiling where price may face selling pressure.
