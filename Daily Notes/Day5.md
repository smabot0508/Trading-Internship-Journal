## Topics Learned

### Moving Average

I learned how Moving Averages help traders identify the overall direction of a trend by smoothing price fluctuations. I understood the difference between Simple Moving Averages (SMA) and Exponential Moving Averages (EMA), and how they are used to determine trend direction and market strength.

I also learned that moving averages can act as dynamic support and resistance levels. During strong uptrends, price often finds support near moving averages, while during downtrends they can act as resistance levels. Another important concept was moving average alignment, where shorter-term moving averages remain above longer-term moving averages, indicating a healthy bullish trend.

---

### Support and Resistance Breakouts

I studied how breakouts occur when price moves beyond significant support or resistance levels. A breakout above resistance often signals increasing buying pressure and the possibility of trend continuation, while a breakdown below support may indicate growing selling pressure.

One of the key lessons was the importance of volume confirmation. Breakouts supported by strong volume are generally more reliable because they indicate participation from traders and institutions. I also learned about breakout retests, where price revisits the breakout level before continuing in the direction of the trend.

---

### Liquidity Introduction

Today I was introduced to the concept of liquidity and its importance in financial markets. Liquidity refers to the ease with which a stock can be bought or sold without significantly affecting its price.

Highly liquid stocks generally have high trading volume, strong market participation, tighter bid-ask spreads, and faster trade execution. I learned that institutional investors often prefer liquid stocks because they can trade large positions more efficiently. Understanding liquidity helps traders evaluate trade execution quality and overall market conditions.
