### Introduction to the US Stock Market

Today I learned the basics of how the US stock market operates and how publicly listed companies allow investors to buy and sell shares. The stock market acts as a marketplace where buyers and sellers trade ownership of companies.

I also learned that stock prices do not move randomly. They are influenced by several important factors:

#### Demand

Demand refers to the willingness of investors to buy a stock. When more traders and investors want to buy a stock, demand increases. Higher demand generally pushes the stock price upward because buyers are willing to pay higher prices.

**Example:**
If many investors believe NVIDIA will perform well in the future, more people will buy its shares, increasing demand and driving the price higher.

#### Supply

Supply refers to the number of shares available for sale in the market. When more investors are willing to sell their shares than buy them, supply increases. Increased supply can cause stock prices to decline.

**Example:**
If many investors decide to sell Tesla shares at the same time, the increased supply may push the stock price lower.

#### Company Performance

A company's financial health and business growth have a significant impact on its stock price. Investors closely monitor factors such as revenue growth, profits, market share, and future business plans.

**Example:**
A company reporting strong profits and business expansion is often viewed positively by investors, which can increase buying interest.

#### News

Stock prices react quickly to important news events. Positive news often attracts buyers, while negative news can create selling pressure.

**Examples of News Events:**

* New product launches
* Major partnerships
* Government regulations
* Economic reports
* Leadership changes

#### Earnings

Earnings represent the profit generated by a company during a specific period. Public companies release earnings reports every quarter, and investors compare actual results with expectations.

Strong earnings often increase investor confidence, while weak earnings may cause the stock price to decline.

#### Market Sentiment

Market sentiment refers to the overall attitude and emotions of investors toward the market or a specific stock.

* Positive sentiment is called **Bullish Sentiment**.
* Negative sentiment is called **Bearish Sentiment**.

Sometimes stock prices move because of investor emotions and expectations even before actual company results are released.
