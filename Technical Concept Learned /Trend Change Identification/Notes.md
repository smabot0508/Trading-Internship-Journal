# Technical Confluences

## Introduction

A technical confluence occurs when multiple trading concepts align at the same area on a chart. Rather than relying on a single indicator or signal, traders combine several factors to increase the probability of a successful trade.

Professional traders rarely take trades based on one reason alone. Instead, they look for multiple confirmations before entering a position.

---

## Why Confluences Matter

A single signal can often produce false setups.

For example:

* A BOS alone may fail.
* A liquidity sweep alone may fail.
* A Fair Value Gap alone may fail.

However, when several concepts align together, the probability of success increases significantly.

---

## Common SMC Confluences

### Break of Structure (BOS)

Provides confirmation that market structure has shifted.

### Liquidity Sweeps

Show where stop losses have been collected before a move.

### Fair Value Gaps (FVG)

Highlight areas of market imbalance.

### Equilibrium

Helps identify premium and discount pricing zones.

### Breaker Blocks

Identify failed order blocks that can become powerful reversal areas.

---

## Example of a High-Probability Setup

A trader may:

1. Identify a liquidity sweep.
2. Observe a Fair Value Gap.
3. Wait for a BOS confirmation.
4. Enter near an equilibrium zone.

When all these factors align, the setup becomes significantly stronger than any individual signal.

---

## Benefits of Using Confluences

* Improves trade quality.
* Reduces false signals.
* Increases confidence.
* Provides clearer trade management.
* Improves risk-to-reward opportunities.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Never trade using a single signal.
* Combine multiple concepts for confirmation.
* BOS becomes stronger when supported by liquidity and FVGs.
* Confluences help create high-probability trading setups.

---

## Conclusion

Technical confluences are what separate professional trading from random decision-making. By combining multiple concepts together, traders can improve their accuracy and make more informed trading decisions.
