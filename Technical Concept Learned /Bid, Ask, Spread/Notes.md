# Bid, Ask, and Spread

## Bid Price

The Bid Price is the highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a stock at a given moment.

Example:

```text
Bid = $99.95
```

This means buyers are currently willing to purchase shares at $99.95.

### Importance

* Represents buying demand.
* Helps traders understand market interest.
* Used in trade execution.

---

## Ask Price

The Ask Price is the lowest price at which a seller is willing to sell a stock.

Example:

```text
Ask = $100.00
```

This means sellers are willing to sell shares at $100.00.

### Importance

* Represents selling supply.
* Determines the purchase price for buyers using market orders.
* Helps measure market activity.

---

## Spread

The Spread is the difference between the Ask Price and the Bid Price.

Formula:

```text
Spread = Ask Price - Bid Price
```

Example:

```text
Ask = $100.00
Bid = $99.95

Spread = $0.05
```

### Importance

* Indicates market liquidity.
* Smaller spreads usually mean higher liquidity.
* Larger spreads often indicate lower liquidity and higher trading costs.

---

## Real-World Trading Application

Professional traders monitor bid, ask, and spread before entering trades.

### Tight Spread

* High liquidity.
* Faster execution.
* Lower transaction costs.

Example:

```text
Bid = $100.00
Ask = $100.01
Spread = $0.01
```

### Wide Spread

* Lower liquidity.
* Higher risk.
* More difficult execution.

Example:

```text
Bid = $100.00
Ask = $101.00
Spread = $1.00
```

Understanding bid, ask, and spread helps traders execute trades efficiently and avoid unnecessary costs.
