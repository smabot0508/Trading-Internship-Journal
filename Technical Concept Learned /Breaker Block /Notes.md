# Breaker Blocks

## Introduction

Breaker Blocks are advanced Smart Money Concepts (SMC) structures that occur when a previously valid Order Block fails and gets broken by price. Instead of becoming useless, the broken Order Block often flips its role and becomes a new area of support or resistance.

Breaker Blocks provide traders with an additional opportunity to enter the market after a market structure shift has occurred. They are commonly used alongside Market Structure, CHOCH, BOS, Liquidity, and Order Blocks to identify high-probability trade setups.

---

## What is a Breaker Block?

A Breaker Block is a previously valid Order Block that has been violated by price movement and is no longer functioning in its original role.

For example:

* A Bullish Order Block acts as support.
* Price breaks below it aggressively.
* Market Structure shifts from bullish to bearish.
* The broken Bullish Order Block becomes a Bearish Breaker Block.

Similarly:

* A Bearish Order Block acts as resistance.
* Price breaks above it.
* Market Structure shifts bullish.
* The broken Bearish Order Block becomes a Bullish Breaker Block.

---

## Why Breaker Blocks Form

Markets constantly seek liquidity.

When a valid Order Block fails, it often indicates that institutions have changed direction or that stronger market participants have entered the market.

The broken Order Block becomes a significant area where price may react when revisited.

This creates a Breaker Block.

---

## Conditions for a Valid Breaker Block

### 1. Valid Order Block Must Exist

A proper Order Block must be present before a Breaker Block can form.

The Order Block should:

* Create an imbalance.
* Remain unmitigated.
* Cause a BOS or CHOCH.

### 2. Market Structure Shift Must Occur

Price must break important structure.

Examples:

* Higher Low breaks → Bearish CHOCH.
* Lower High breaks → Bullish CHOCH.

### 3. Order Block Must Be Broken

The market must aggressively move through the Order Block.

This confirms that the original institutional zone has failed.

### 4. Price Must Retrace

After the break, price usually revisits the broken Order Block.

This retracement creates the Breaker Block trading opportunity.

---

## Bullish Breaker Block

A Bullish Breaker Block forms when:

1. Bearish Order Block exists.
2. Price breaks above it.
3. CHOCH confirms bullish structure.
4. Price retraces back.
5. Broken resistance acts as support.

Traders look for buying opportunities from this area.

---

## Bearish Breaker Block

A Bearish Breaker Block forms when:

1. Bullish Order Block exists.
2. Price breaks below it.
3. CHOCH confirms bearish structure.
4. Price retraces back.
5. Broken support acts as resistance.

Traders look for selling opportunities from this area.

---

## Breaker Block Trading Process

### Step 1

Identify a valid Order Block.

### Step 2

Wait for price to break through it.

### Step 3

Confirm a CHOCH.

### Step 4

Mark the broken Order Block.

### Step 5

Wait for price retracement.

### Step 6

Look for confirmation signals.

### Step 7

Enter the trade with proper risk management.

---

## Risk Management

* Place stop loss beyond the Breaker Block.
* Target the next liquidity zone.
* Maintain at least 2:1 Risk-to-Reward.
* Wait for confirmation before entering.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Breaker Blocks are failed Order Blocks.
* They form after a CHOCH.
* Broken support becomes resistance.
* Broken resistance becomes support.
* They provide additional trading opportunities after market structure shifts.
* Breaker Blocks work best when combined with liquidity and market structure analysis.

---

## Conclusion

Breaker Blocks help traders capitalize on failed Order Blocks and market structure shifts. By understanding how institutions reposition themselves after a trend reversal, traders can identify high-probability areas where price is likely to react. Combined with BOS, CHOCH, and liquidity analysis, Breaker Blocks become a powerful tool within Smart Money Concepts.
