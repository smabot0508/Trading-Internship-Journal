# Equilibrium Level

## Introduction

The Equilibrium Level is the exact midpoint of a price range and serves as the foundation of the Premium and Discount concept.

It represents the 50% level between a swing high and swing low and separates expensive pricing from cheap pricing.

---

## What is Equilibrium?

Equilibrium is simply:

* 50% of a price range.
* The midpoint between buyers and sellers.
* The dividing line between Premium and Discount.

Above equilibrium:

* Premium pricing.

Below equilibrium:

* Discount pricing.

---

## Why Equilibrium is Important

The Equilibrium Level helps traders determine:

* Whether price is expensive.
* Whether price is cheap.
* Whether a trade entry is premature.
* Whether a supply or demand zone is in a favorable location.

---

## Trading with Equilibrium

### Bullish Markets

Wait for price to retrace below equilibrium into Discount before buying.

### Bearish Markets

Wait for price to retrace above equilibrium into Premium before selling.

---

## Benefits

Equilibrium helps traders:

* Improve entry quality.
* Reduce emotional decisions.
* Avoid chasing price.
* Improve risk-to-reward ratios.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Equilibrium is the 50% midpoint.
* Above = Premium.
* Below = Discount.
* Helps filter poor entries.
* Works best with market structure.

---

## Conclusion

The Equilibrium Level is a simple but powerful concept that provides objective pricing context. It helps traders avoid poor trade locations and focus only on high-probability opportunities.
