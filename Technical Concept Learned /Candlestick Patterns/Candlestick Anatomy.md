# Candlestick Anatomy

## Introduction

Candlestick Anatomy is the study of the individual components that make up a candlestick on a price chart. Understanding candlestick anatomy is essential because market structure, trend analysis, and Break of Structure (BOS) identification all depend on correctly reading candlestick movements.

Each candlestick represents price activity during a specific period and provides valuable information about buyer and seller behavior.

---

## Components of a Candlestick

Every candlestick consists of two primary parts:

### 1. Candle Body

The body represents the distance between the opening price and closing price.

* Green (Bullish) Candle: Closing price is higher than opening price.
* Red (Bearish) Candle: Closing price is lower than opening price.

The size of the body often indicates the strength of buying or selling pressure during that period.

### 2. Candle Wicks

Wicks (also called shadows) extend above and below the candle body.

* Upper Wick: Highest price reached during the period.
* Lower Wick: Lowest price reached during the period.

Wicks show areas where price moved temporarily but failed to maintain those levels.

---

## How Highs and Lows Are Formed

In market structure analysis, highs and lows are created through candle sequences.

### Formation of a High

A high forms when:

* An up candle is followed by a down candle.

The highest point reached by either candle becomes the official swing high.

### Formation of a Low

A low forms when:

* A down candle is followed by an up candle.

The lowest point reached by either candle becomes the official swing low.

---

## Wicks vs Body Closures

One of the most important concepts in SMC is understanding the difference between a wick and a body close.

### Wick Break

Price temporarily moves beyond a level but closes back inside the structure.

This does NOT confirm a trend change.

### Body Close

The candle closes completely beyond the structural level.

This confirms a valid Break of Structure (BOS).

Many traders make mistakes by reacting to wick breaks instead of waiting for body confirmation.

---

## Why Candlestick Anatomy Matters

Candlestick anatomy helps traders:

* Identify market structure.
* Confirm BOS.
* Recognize trend changes.
* Understand buyer and seller strength.
* Avoid false breakouts.

Without understanding candlestick behavior, traders may misinterpret market movements and make poor trading decisions.

---

## Key Takeaways

* Every candlestick contains a body and wicks.
* Bodies represent open and close prices.
* Wicks represent temporary price movement.
* Highs and lows are formed through candle sequences.
* BOS confirmation requires body closures, not wick breaks.

---

## Conclusion

Candlestick Anatomy forms the foundation of price action analysis. By understanding how candle bodies and wicks behave, traders can better identify market structure, trend changes, and high-probability trading opportunities.
