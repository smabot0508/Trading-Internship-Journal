# SMC Practice – Change of Character (ChoCh) & Market Structure Shift

During today's practical session, I focused on identifying Change of Character (ChoCh) formations and understanding how market structure shifts occur after a valid reversal. I practiced observing price action around important supply and demand zones and analyzed how market momentum changes when buyers or sellers begin losing control.

I learned that a ChoCh acts as the earliest warning sign of a potential trend reversal. Rather than immediately assuming a new trend has started, I observed how additional confirmations such as higher timeframe mitigation, liquidity sweeps, and double zone breakouts increase the probability of a successful setup.

I also practiced distinguishing between valid and invalid ChoCh formations. A key lesson was that not every structural break represents a genuine reversal. Price must first interact with an important higher timeframe supply or demand zone before a ChoCh can be considered reliable.

Another important concept I practiced was identifying liquidity sweeps before reversals. I observed how equal highs, equal lows, and double tops often act as liquidity pools that are targeted before price changes direction. This helped me better understand why many traders are trapped before the actual move begins.

Finally, I studied the difference between extreme zones and decisional zones after a double zone breakout. I learned that extreme zones generally provide safer entries because decisional zones are frequently swept before the market continues in the intended direction.

This practical session strengthened my understanding of institutional market behavior and improved my ability to identify high-probability reversal setups using Smart Money Concepts.
