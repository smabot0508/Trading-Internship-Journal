# Liquidity Practice

During today's practical session, I applied liquidity concepts directly on real stock charts. The objective was to identify areas where significant liquidity exists and observe how price reacts around those levels.

I analyzed previous highs, previous lows, swing highs, swing lows, and consolidation zones to understand where pending orders and stop-losses may be concentrated. I observed that price frequently moves toward these areas before either reversing or continuing its trend.

This exercise helped me connect theoretical liquidity concepts with actual market behavior. Instead of viewing liquidity as an abstract idea, I was able to identify liquidity zones directly on charts and understand how they influence price movement.

Through this practice, I improved my ability to recognize important market levels and gained a better understanding of how liquidity affects trading decisions and market dynamics.
