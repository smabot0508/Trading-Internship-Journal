# TradingView Practice – Premium & Discount

During today's practical session, I practiced applying the Premium and Discount concept using the Fibonacci Retracement tool on TradingView. The objective was to identify whether price was trading at expensive or cheap levels within a specific market structure range.

I began by identifying the overall market structure and locating the swing high and swing low responsible for the current trend. After determining the range, I applied the Fibonacci Retracement tool and focused on the 50% Equilibrium Level.

The exercise showed how price behaves differently when trading above and below equilibrium. When price entered the Premium zone, I observed that supply zones often became more effective areas for potential selling opportunities. Similarly, when price entered the Discount zone, demand zones provided stronger buying opportunities.

I also practiced comparing multiple supply and demand zones within the same range. This helped me understand why extreme zones often provide stronger reactions than zones located closer to equilibrium.

Through Bar Replay mode, I tested historical chart data and observed how combining market structure, supply and demand, and Premium/Discount analysis improved trade selection. This practice reinforced the importance of waiting for favorable pricing rather than entering trades prematurely.

### Key Observations

- Premium represents expensive pricing.
- Discount represents cheap pricing.
- Equilibrium acts as the dividing line.
- Demand zones work best in Discount.
- Supply zones work best in Premium.
- Extreme zones often provide stronger reactions.
- Patience improves trade quality.
