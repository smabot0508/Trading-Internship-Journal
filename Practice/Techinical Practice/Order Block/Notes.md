# SMC Practice – Order Blocks

During today's practical session, I applied the Order Block concepts learned in the Smart Money Concepts module by analyzing real market charts and identifying potential institutional trading zones. I focused on locating strong impulsive price movements and then marking the final candle before the move as a potential Order Block.

While practicing, I verified each setup using the three validation rules. First, I checked whether the move created a clear imbalance or Fair Value Gap (FVG). Second, I ensured the Order Block had not been mitigated or revisited by price after its creation. Third, I confirmed that the move eventually resulted in either a Break of Structure (BOS) or a Change of Character (CHOCH), validating the institutional significance of the zone.

I also observed how price often revisits these Order Blocks before continuing in the intended direction. This helped me understand why institutions use these areas to enter or add to positions. During the analysis, I practiced identifying bullish and bearish Order Blocks and compared them with traditional support and resistance zones to understand the differences.

Additionally, I studied examples of inducement traps where price temporarily moved beyond minor support or resistance levels to trigger retail stop losses before returning to the Order Block area. This reinforced the relationship between liquidity, smart money behavior, and institutional order flow.

I further explored Breaker Blocks by observing situations where previously valid Order Blocks failed and were broken by price. After a CHOCH occurred, these broken Order Blocks acted as new support or resistance zones, creating additional trading opportunities.

This practical exercise improved my ability to identify institutional footprints on a chart, validate Order Blocks correctly, understand market structure shifts, and apply Smart Money Concepts more effectively in real trading scenarios.
