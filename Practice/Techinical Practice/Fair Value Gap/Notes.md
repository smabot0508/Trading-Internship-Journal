# Technical Practice – Fair Value Gap (FVG)

## Objective

The objective of today's practice session was to identify Fair Value Gaps (FVGs) on a live chart and understand how market imbalance, Break of Structure (BOS), and support levels work together to create high-probability trading opportunities.

---

## Chart Analysis

During the practice session, I analyzed the S&P 500 (SPX) chart on the 5-minute timeframe and identified a bullish Fair Value Gap created after a strong impulsive bullish move.

The chart showed a clear Break of Structure where price successfully broke above a previous high. This aggressive upward movement created a market imbalance, leaving behind an unfilled Fair Value Gap zone.

I marked the FVG by identifying the gap between the surrounding candles and highlighted the area where price could potentially retrace before continuing higher.

---

## Observations

### 1. Break of Structure (BOS)

Before the Fair Value Gap was created, price broke a previous structural high.

This Break of Structure confirmed that buyers had gained control of the market and increased the probability of the bullish setup.

### 2. Fair Value Gap Formation

A strong bullish impulse candle moved rapidly upward, creating an imbalance between buyers and sellers.

Because the move happened so quickly, the market left behind a Fair Value Gap that could later act as a support zone.

### 3. Support Confluence

The Fair Value Gap aligned closely with a previously identified support level.

This confluence increased the strength of the setup because multiple technical factors were supporting the same area.

### 4. Unmitigated Gap

At the time of analysis, the Fair Value Gap had not yet been fully tested by price.

This meant the gap remained fresh and unmitigated, making it a higher-quality trading zone.

---

## Key Factors Identified

While analyzing the chart, I verified the following important FVG conditions:

* Fair Value Gap was unmitigated.
* A clear Break of Structure occurred before the gap.
* Support aligned with the FVG zone.
* The gap was located in a strong bullish move.
* The imbalance was clearly visible.
* Price respected the market structure.

These factors increased the probability of a successful reaction if price returned to the zone.

---

## What I Learned

Through this practice session, I learned that not every Fair Value Gap is worth trading. The strongest setups occur when multiple confirmations align together.

I also understood that:

* Market Structure provides directional bias.
* Break of Structure confirms momentum.
* Fair Value Gaps identify areas of imbalance.
* Support and Resistance improve trade quality.
* Fresh, unmitigated gaps are generally more reliable.

This practical analysis helped me understand how professional traders combine FVGs with market structure rather than trading gaps in isolation.

---

## Conclusion

Today's Fair Value Gap practice demonstrated how market imbalances are formed and how they can provide high-quality trading opportunities when combined with Break of Structure and support levels. By identifying unmitigated gaps, confirming market structure, and waiting for price to revisit the imbalance zone, traders can improve their trade selection and overall market analysis.
