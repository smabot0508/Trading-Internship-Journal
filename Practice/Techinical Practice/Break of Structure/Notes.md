# SMC Practice – BOS & Market Structure

During today's practical session, I applied the concepts of Market Structure, Break of Structure (BOS), Trend Identification, and Candlestick Anatomy using real price charts. The objective was to move beyond theory and develop the ability to recognize structural changes directly from market behavior.

I began by identifying Higher Highs (HH), Higher Lows (HL), Lower Highs (LH), and Lower Lows (LL) on different chart timeframes. This helped me understand how trends are formed and how buyers and sellers interact throughout the market cycle.

While analyzing the charts, I observed that an uptrend remains valid as long as price continues creating Higher Highs and Higher Lows. Similarly, a downtrend remains intact while Lower Highs and Lower Lows continue to form. This exercise improved my ability to determine the overall market direction without relying on indicators.

A major part of the practice involved identifying valid Breaks of Structure (BOS). I carefully monitored key structural highs and lows and learned that a BOS is only confirmed when a candlestick body closes beyond the important structural level. Several examples showed price temporarily breaking levels with wicks before reversing back into the structure. These situations reinforced the importance of waiting for candle body confirmation rather than reacting to temporary price spikes.

I also practiced identifying trend changes by monitoring the most recent Higher Low during an uptrend and the most recent Lower High during a downtrend. This process helped me understand how market structure shifts from bullish to bearish conditions and vice versa.

Another important lesson from today's practice was understanding the relationship between major and minor market structure. By observing multiple timeframes, I noticed how smaller corrections on lower timeframes often exist within larger trends on higher timeframes. This demonstrated the importance of multi-timeframe analysis when making trading decisions.

Finally, I explored how BOS should be combined with additional technical confluences rather than traded in isolation. Market structure becomes significantly more reliable when supported by concepts such as liquidity, Fair Value Gaps (FVGs), and other Smart Money Concepts.

This practical session strengthened my ability to read market structure objectively, identify trend direction, recognize valid structural breaks, and apply SMC principles using real chart examples.
