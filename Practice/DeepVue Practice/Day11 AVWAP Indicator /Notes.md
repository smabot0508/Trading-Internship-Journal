# DeepVue Practice – Anchored VWAP (AVWAP)

During today's practical session, I explored the Anchored Volume Weighted Average Price (AVWAP) indicator within DeepVue and learned how traders use it to identify market control, support, resistance, and trend strength.

Using NVIDIA (NVDA) as the practice stock, I observed how the AVWAP line adjusted based on the selected anchor point and how price reacted around the indicator throughout different phases of the trend.

I learned that when the stock remained above the AVWAP line, buyers maintained control and the indicator often acted as dynamic support. When price approached the AVWAP during pullbacks, the level frequently served as an area where buyers could re-enter the market.

I also observed how AVWAP can be anchored to important market events such as major lows, significant reversals, earnings gaps, or breakout points. This allows traders to track institutional positioning from a specific moment in time and better understand whether buying or selling pressure is dominating.

Through this practice, I understood that AVWAP is more than a moving average. It combines both price and volume, making it a valuable tool for identifying trend direction, support and resistance levels, and potential entry opportunities. The exercise demonstrated how professional traders use AVWAP alongside market structure and volume analysis to improve decision-making and risk management.
