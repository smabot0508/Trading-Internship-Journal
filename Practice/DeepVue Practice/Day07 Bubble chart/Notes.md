# DeepVue Bubble Chart

## Overview

The DeepVue Bubble Chart is a real-time market visualization tool designed to help traders quickly identify breakout stocks, market leaders, sector strength, and unusual trading activity.

Unlike traditional stock screeners that display data in rows and columns, the Bubble Chart presents information visually using interactive bubbles. This allows traders to analyze multiple stocks simultaneously and identify opportunities more efficiently.

---

## Purpose

The primary purpose of the Bubble Chart is to:

* Find breakout stocks quickly.
* Identify market leaders.
* Monitor sector strength.
* Detect unusual volume activity.
* Improve stock screening efficiency.

The tool helps traders focus on stocks that are actively in play rather than manually reviewing hundreds of charts.

---

## How the Bubble Chart Works

The Bubble Chart uses three separate data points:

### X-Axis

The X-Axis controls the horizontal position of bubbles.

Common metrics include:

* Price Percentage Change
* Relative Strength (RS)
* Momentum Indicators

Stocks move further to the right when they perform better according to the selected metric.

---

### Y-Axis

The Y-Axis controls the vertical position of bubbles.

Common metrics include:

* Relative Volume
* Relative Strength
* Performance Metrics

Stocks move higher on the chart when their selected metric increases.

---

### Bubble Size

Bubble size represents a third variable.

Many traders use Relative Volume because larger bubbles make it easier to identify stocks receiving increased market attention.

---

## Finding Breakouts

One of the most popular Bubble Chart configurations uses:

### X-Axis

Price Percentage Change from Open

### Y-Axis

Relative Volume

### Bubble Size

Relative Volume (20-Day Basis)

Using this setup:

* Strong stocks move toward the right side.
* High-volume stocks move upward.
* Larger bubbles indicate stronger participation.

Stocks appearing in the top-right area of the chart are often considered potential breakout candidates.

---

## Identifying Market Leaders

The Bubble Chart can help traders discover future market leaders by combining:

### Relative Strength (RS)

Relative Strength measures how strongly a stock is performing compared to the market or other stocks.

Higher Relative Strength often indicates leadership characteristics.

---

### Relative Measured Volatility (RMV)

Relative Measured Volatility measures how tightly a stock is trading before making a larger move.

Stocks showing:

* High Relative Strength
* Tight Volatility
* Increasing Volume

may have the potential to become future market leaders.

---

## Sector Analysis

The Bubble Chart helps traders identify sector rotation and institutional money flow.

For example:

If multiple semiconductor stocks appear in the top-right area of the chart, it may indicate strength within the semiconductor sector.

This allows traders to focus on strong industries and market themes.

---

## Key Features

### Interactive Bubbles

Clicking a bubble instantly updates the linked stock chart for deeper analysis.

### Real-Time Updates

The chart updates continuously during market hours.

### Zoom and Navigation

Traders can zoom into specific areas of the chart to focus on selected groups of stocks.

### Linked Chart Analysis

Selecting a stock from the Bubble Chart immediately displays its chart, making detailed analysis faster.
