# DeepVue Practice – 3 Weeks Tight Pattern

During today's practical session, I explored the 3 Weeks Tight Pattern using the DeepVue platform and practiced identifying the setup on a real stock chart.

For this exercise, I analyzed PEB (Pebblebrook Hotel Trust) on the weekly timeframe. The chart displayed a clear period of tight price consolidation followed by a strong breakout move.

I observed that several consecutive weekly candles traded within a relatively narrow range, indicating reduced volatility and price contraction. This behavior suggested that selling pressure was being absorbed while stronger market participants continued accumulating shares.

After the consolidation period, the stock produced a large bullish breakout candle accompanied by increased volume. The breakout demonstrated the principle that periods of contraction often lead to expansion.

The chart also showed that the breakout occurred above a well-defined support area near $14.79, providing additional confirmation of the setup.

Through this practice, I learned how professional traders use the 3 Weeks Tight Pattern to identify stocks showing institutional accumulation before major price advances. The exercise improved my ability to recognize tight consolidations, analyze volume confirmation, and identify potential breakout opportunities using DeepVue.
